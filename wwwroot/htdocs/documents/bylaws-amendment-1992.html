Mainlands Seven Maintenance Corporation

Amendment to the By-Laws

WHEREAS, MAINLANDS SEVEN MAINTENANCE CORPORATION WAS CREATED AND IS MAINTAINED AS A RETIREMENT COMMUNITY FOR OLDER PERSONS, AND

WHEREAS, IN EXCESS OF 80% OF ALL RESIDENTIAL UNITS ARE NOW OCCUPIED BY AT LEAST ONE RESIDENT OVER THE AGE OF 55, AND

WHEREAS, OUR COMMUNITY HAS SIGNIFICANT FACILITIES AND SERVICES FOR THE BENEFIT OF RESIDENTS OVER THE AGE OF 55, AND

WHEREAS, THE FAIR HOUSING AMENDMENTS ACT OF 1988 PROHIBITS DISCRIMINATION IN THE SALE OR RENTAL OF HOUSING TO INDIVIDUALS BELOW THE AGE OF (18) EIGHTEEN LIVING WITH A PARENT OR LEGAL CUSTODIAN, UNLESS THE COMMUNITY QUALIFIES FOR AN EXEMPTION BY PROVIDING HOUSING FOR "OLDER PERSONS", AND

WHEREAS, TO REMAIN EXEMPTED FROM THE APPLICATION OF THE FAIR HOUSING AMENDEMENT OF 1988, THE COMMUNITY MUST MAINTAIN A RESIDENTIAL AND RENTAL RATIO OF NOT LESS THAN 80% OF ITS HOMES OCCUPIED BY AT LEAST ONE RESIDENT AGE 55 OR OLDER, AND

WHEREAS, A GENERAL MEETING OF THE HOMEOWNERS OF MAINLANDS SEVEN MAINTENANCE CORPORATION WAS HELD ON NOVEMBER 20, 1992 TO VOTE ON A PROPOSED AMENDMENT TO AMEND THE BY-LAWS TO CONFORM WITH THE FAIR HOUSING ACT OF 1988, AND

WHEREAS, 2/3 OF A QUORUM OF MEMBERS PRESENT IN PERSON OR ABSENTEE BALLOTS TO AMEND THE BY-LAWS AS FOLLOWS:

THAT THE MAINLANDS SEVEN MAINTENANCE CORPORATION DEED RESTRICTIONS AND BY-LAWS RECORDED IN:

  1. BK3896 PAGE 185
  2. BK7505 PAGE 162

PUBLIC RECORDS OF BROWARD COUNTY, FLORIDA BE AMENDED TO STATE THAT:

(A) "AT LEAST ONE PERSON 55 YEARS OR OLDER OCCUPY EACH UNIT AND THAT PERMANENT RESIDENCE IS RESTRICTED TO PERSONS OVER 18 YEARS OF AGE.

(B) PRESENT HOMEOWNERS UNDER THE AGE OF 55 AND ALL MINOR CHILDREN PRESENTLY RESIDING IN SECTION 7 ARE "GRANDFATHERED IN" AND ARE NOT AFFECTED BY THE MODIFICATION OF THE BY-LAWS.

(C) THE BOARD HAS THE POWER TO WAIVE DEED RESTRICTIONS IN HARDSHIP CASES.

(D) THE BOARD WILL ESTABLISH A SCREENING COMMITTEE CONSISTING OF AT LEAST THREE MEMBERS TO INSURE THAT NEW HOMEOWNERS OR RENTERS ARE IN COMPLIANCE WITH OUR AMENDED BY LAWS.
